What is a stock?

A stock, also known as equity, is a security that represents the ownership of a fraction of the issuing corporation. Units of stock are called "shares" which entitles the owner to a proportion of the corporation's assets and profits equal to how much stock they own. Stocks are bought and sold predominantly on stock exchanges and are the foundation of many individual investors' portfolios. Stock trades have to conform to government regulations meant to protect investors from fraudulent practices.

How do i invest in the stock markets of India?

Stocks are usually a hit and miss type of thing as an investor you need to find best stocks to invest in. The buying and selling procedure of the stock is very easy as compared to any other investment options. Hence, investing in stock is more like an ideal investment for a lot of people The amount of money you want to invest in depends on you. These are the points you should remember when you are investing in stocks in India.

1.Find a Broker: You cannot directly go the stock exchange and buy or sell stocks/shares. People are authorized to buy and sell on the markets and they are called brokers. Brokers can be individuals or companies and even online agencies that are registered and licensed by SEBI. A broker, they can be individuals you know and are reliable, or you can approach various companies that are licensed to trade and deal in securities in the markets.

2.Learn Trading in Stocks: Before investing you should know every technical and fundamental aspect of trading stocks. You should also make up your mind if you want to invest in a long term stock or a short term stock. To learn trading perfectly you can practice trading on trade simulators. Such simulators are available on many investing websites. In the real world to learn investing in stocks without losing much money is to try investing in some blue-chip stocks.

3.Invest in Companies that you understand.If you understand a business, or a sector, you could have the upper hand when it comes to buying its stock. Your experience may not be financially related, but with the ability to invest in everything, your knowledge might come handy in the stock market. As Warren Buffet talks about personal investing rules, he says "if you don’t understand a business, do not buy it."

4.Invest regularly: Invest regularly and methodically and let the magic of compounding work for you.

5.Never time the market: Be a smart investor. Always invest in time but never try to time the market. Timing the market is mastered by none and is beyond one's control.

6.Be patient: For long-term wealth creation, you need to be patient. The longer the investment horizon, the lesser is the risk and greater are the returns.

7.Open your account and start investing.There are many options to open an account make an investment portfolio and start investing. After you open your account, as a beginner you can choose an online portfolio management company that can help you manage your account and also give you insider advisory. There are also offers with many brokers to open a free account, which can be a great start.

8.Keep these points in your mind and you might just be starting to change to a pro trader from beginner among everyone.
